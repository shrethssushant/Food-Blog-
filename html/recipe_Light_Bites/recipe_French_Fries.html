French Fries

Golden, crispy French fries are a delightful snack made from thinly sliced potatoes, fried to perfection and lightly salted, offering a satisfying crunch with every bite.

Ingredients


  • 2 large Russet or Yukon Gold potatoes - These varieties are perfect for achieving that ideal crispiness.

  • Vegetable oil - Approximately 4 cups, or enough to fully submerge the fries during frying.

  • Salt - To taste, for that essential flavor boost.

  • Optional: Garlic powder, paprika, or your favorite seasoning for an extra kick.

Tip: Choose firm potatoes and store them in a cool, dark place to maintain freshness.

Instructions


  1. Prep: Peel and cut the potatoes into uniform 1/4-inch strips. Soak them in cold water for at least 30 minutes to remove excess starch.

  2. First Fry: Heat the oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 325°F (165°C). Fry the potatoes in batches for 3-4 minutes until they are soft but not browned. Drain them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.

  3. Second Fry: Increase the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Fry the potatoes again in batches for 2-3 minutes until they are golden brown and crispy. Drain and season with salt while hot.

  4. Serve: Enjoy your fries hot, paired with your favorite dipping sauces like ketchup, mayo, or aioli!

Chef's Tip: For an extra flavor boost, sprinkle with garlic powder or paprika right after frying for a delicious twist!
