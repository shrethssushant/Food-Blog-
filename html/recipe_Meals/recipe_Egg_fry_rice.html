About Us

"Egg fried rice is the most simple and delicious dish. You take day-old rice—not freshly cooked, haiyaa—and fry it in a hot wok with oil. Then, you crack egg straight into the wok, scramble it nicely, and mix with the rice. Add garlic, spring onion, soy sauce, and of course, MSG—the king of flavor! No peas, no chili jam, no nonsense!Cook on high heat for that perfect wok hei. Simple, fast, and not wet! If your fried rice is wet, you have failed at life!"

Ingredients


  • Cooked rice - 3 cups of cooked rice (Day-old rice)

  • Vegetables - 1 cup of mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots, bell peppers)

  • Egg - 2 whole

  • Soy Sauce - 2-3 tablespoons of soy sauce

  • Sesame Oil - 1 tablespoon of sesame oil

  • Garlic - 2 cloves of garlic, minced

  • Green onions - 2 green onions, chopped

Tip: Use cold rice, high heat, and soy sauce.

Instructions


  1. Prepare the ingredients: Chop vegetables like carrots, bell peppers, peas, and spring onions.

  2. Cook the rice: Boil rice until almost cooked, then drain and let it cool.

  3. Heat oil: In a pan, heat some oil and add chopped garlic and ginger.

  4. Fry the egg: Fry 2 eggs in the same pan.

  5. Add vegetables: Stir-fry the vegetables and until cooked but still crisp.

  6. Add rice: Add the cooked rice to the pan and mix well with the vegetables.

  7. Season: Add soy sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Optionally, add a little vinegar.

  8. Mix thoroughly: Stir the rice and vegetables well to combine all flavors.

  9. Garnish: Top with chopped spring onions before serving.

Chef's Tip: Use cold, day-old rice for the best fried rice.
