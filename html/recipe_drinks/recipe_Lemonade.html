Lemonade

Lemonade is a refreshing and tangy beverage made from fresh lemon juice, water, and sugar. It's the perfect drink for hot summer days or whenever you need a sweet and citrusy pick-me-up. Whether served chilled over ice or garnished with mint or fruit, lemonade is a timeless favorite enjoyed by people of all ages.

Ingredients


  • Fresh lemons – 4 (for about 1 cup of juice)

  • Sugar – 1 cup (adjust to taste)

  • Water – 4 cups (divided into 2 cups of hot water and 2 cups of cold water)

  • Ice cubes – For serving

  • Fresh mint leaves – Optional, for garnish

Tip: For a twist, add a few slices of fresh ginger or cucumber to your lemonade.

Instructions


  1. Juice the lemons: Roll the lemons on the counter to make them juicier, then cut them in half and squeeze out the juice into a bowl. You should get about 1 cup of fresh lemon juice.

  2. Dissolve the sugar: In a separate bowl or pitcher, combine the sugar with the 2 cups of hot water. Stir until the sugar is completely dissolved.

  3. Mix the lemonade: Add the fresh lemon juice to the sugar-water mixture, then stir in the 2 cups of cold water.

  4. Chill and serve: Fill glasses with ice cubes and pour the lemonade over. Garnish with mint leaves if desired. Enjoy!

Chef's Tip: If you prefer a less sweet lemonade, reduce the sugar or substitute it with honey or agave syrup.
